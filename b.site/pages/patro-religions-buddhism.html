Buddhism

Buddhism is the fourth largest religion of the world falling behind Christianity, Islam and the Hindus. Over 520 million people of the world are Buddhists. The religion was founded by Siddhartha Gautam also known as "Gautam Buddha". The East and SouthEast Asia have been prominent region for the Buddhism. The religion is also influencing the west. China has the largest number of Buddhists people whereas the religion is followed worldwide. Buddhism is a non-theistic religion as no gods are worshipped in Buddhism. The Buddhists people look up to Buddha with respect but do not believe him as the god but they worship him in the form of respect. Buddhists people worship at home or their temple. They sit on the floor barefoot and chant. They always face an image while chanting but it is important that their feet faces away from the image/statue of the Buddha.

Origin

Siddhartha Gautam was a prince born around 480 BCE in the present-day Kapilvastu district. He was the king of the son and very wealthy. He had an easy life as he spent his childhood inside the kingdom only. When he was in his adulthood, he visited outside of the kingdom for the first time and was moved by suffering in the world. He was unknown of being old, death and so many other things. The first visit outside the kingdom made him realized the cruelty of the world. So, he decided to leave his kingdom and his lifestyle and went away. After six years, Buddhists believe Siddhartha Gautam found enlightenment while meditating under a Bodhi tree in Gaya. Then, he was known as Gautam Buddha and he spent the rest of his life teaching how to achieve spiritual state. He passed away around 400 BCE, the community he had created with his teachings slowly evolved. This community turned into a religion and was known as the Buddhism. The teachings of Buddha became the basis of Buddhism.

Deities (Gods & Goddesses)

Buddhism is a non-thiestic religion i.e. there is no belief in personal God. Gautam Buddha is respected and worshipped. The people in Buddhism chant or worship infront of his image/ statue. But Buddha is also not considered as the God. It was the teachings of Buddha that became the basis for the religion Buddhism. The people do not consider anyone as the God or deity.

Festivals

There are many Buddhist festivals celebrated around the globe. Most of them marks the events in Buddha's life. Some of them are:

  • Buddha Day: The main festival of the year for Buddhists is Buddha Day also known as "Wesak" or "Vaisakha". It marks the birthday, elightenment day and death of Gautam Buddha. Buddhists people decorate their houses. Lanterns are made and the people visit their local temples and give offerings to the monks.
  • Magha Pooja: The second most important festival is celebrated remembering the meeting between Gautam Buddha and his first 1250 disciples. It is celebrated as a chance for people of the Buddhism to reavouch their commitment to their religion. It is also known as "Sangha Day" because it celebrates ideal of creating a community and also the community that they are trying to create.
  • Losar: The festival is celebrated by the Tibetan Buddhists and is also one of the main festivals in Nepal. It embarks the beginning of the New year in the Buddhist calendar.

Holy Books

Buddhists have many hooly books and scriptures. Although, the sacred book of Buddhism is called the Tripitaka. Other important books are Mahayana Sutras ("The word of Buddha") and Book of the Dead. Mahayana Sutras includes the teachings of Buddha to his pupils while the Book of the Dead states the stages of death. Whereas, Tripitaka is considered to be the earliest book and record of the words of Buddha.
